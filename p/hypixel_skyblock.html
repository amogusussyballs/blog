Introduction

Hypixel Skyblock is a MMORPG gamemode within Hypixel, which is a server in Minecraft. Known for its interesting multiplayer and deviant spin of regular Skyblock, Hypixel Skyblock contains custom enemies, items, and dungeons. But honestly, I didn't care about all of that: I cared about forms of financial exchange - the auction house and the bazaar.

Finanical Exchange

The auction house is where players can list their items and people can bid on it. As a semi-automated system, there are some mishaps (items being sold for too little, too high) for valuation of items. There are two types of auctions: regular auction and Buy-it-now (BIN) auctions. Regular auctions are what they imply: players bidding each other out until the final bid value. BIN auctions are where an item can be bought instantly if the listed price is paid. While BIN auctions were introduced later, they are now much more popular. This is probably since the risk of an item being sold with a low bid is reduced. BIN auctions can expire but they can't be sold for lower than the listed price, which is reassuring to most people.

The bazaar is exactly like a stock market, except the items are the "stock". There are buy and sell orders and spreads! We'll explore this later on. However, only commodities (ex: wheat, mutton, ...) can only be traded, which does not include skins and most other items. Since it's so inconvenient, and I'm not experienced enough to talk about it, I'll probably sav eit for a later post.

Skyblock Firesale Index Fund

Trying to replicate index funds in the real world, I created the Hypixel Skyblock Firesale Total Market Index Fund. Firesales are where limited quantities of "skins" are sold, similar to how stock is issued at an IPO. Now, we can calculate market capitalization as

MktCap=total shares⋅price per share.\text{MktCap} = \text{total shares} \cdot \text{price per share}.MktCap=total shares⋅price per share.

Replace share with skin, and one can calculate market cap for a skin. Now, for index funds, it is usually weighted by market cap (meaning if some stock's market cap is 10% of total market cap, it will compose 10% of the portfolio). This means that the work of every investor in the market, every piece of information is accounted for. Pretty interesting.

I find it astonishing that a market in a gamemode in a server for a game can model the real world so accurately. My interest in finance started with Hypixel Skyblock, and it is fulfilling to go back to my roots.

One of my fondest memories is selling Piercing 1 books on the auction house for upwards of 50,000 coins per, when they cost like a hundred coins to make. To craft the books, a high cactus collection level was required, so it was pretty scarce. The whole ordeal taught me about cash flow and cost of goods sold (COGS) and even scarcity.

Starting a Skyblock investing fund may not have been such a bad idea. Even accounting for inflation (in Skyblock), the sheer amount of gains (100x) would have created massive profits. Maybe high-frequency trading could've worked well - actually nevermind, there's an exorbiant amount of fees. Macro-trading and using real quantitative strategies would probably work wonders. Just like how technical indicators worked when no one could calculate them fast enough (before computers), bringing such "serious" techniques from the real financial world could revolutionize the Skyblock markets by creating better valuations and even opportunities for arbitrage. The market is still inefficient for Skyblock.

I'm probably taking Skyblock too seriously. Why am I so fixated on the niche auction house and skins when I am supposed to be fun playing the actual game. I don't know.
