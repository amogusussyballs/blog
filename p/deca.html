Introduction

Last year, I competed in the DECA Business Finance individual series for the first time. In essence, participants have a test they have to take and 2 "roleplay" presentation. For the test, they solve 100 multiple choice problems with 4 options in 90 minutes. There are tests for each area, not each event: so finance events (such as accounting, and of course business finance) have a finance test that is the same. For the two roleplays, participants are given 10 minutes to prepare, and another 10 minutes to present. For business finance, there are 5 KPIs (key performance indicators) that must be explained, and an event situation. Judges usually ask 2 questions at the end.

Strategy

Each component is graded equally, which means that each roleplay and the one test contribute 1/3 to the total score. Therefore, the two roleplays contribute 2/3 to the total score, so they may seem more important. The roleplays are important, but there are some weird gimmicks with the grading of them. The test may be more important, especially at ICDC (the international competition).

For the test, participants have to get at least 80 to be competitive. Above 90 is best, and all it takes is a lot of grinding. There's really nothing else to the test, other than learning some specific content (such as financial law and legal forms).

The roleplay is the most subjective part. Since participants are spread across multiple judges, one judge may grade much harsher than the other. There is an internal factoring system that is used, but it is flawed, especially at States.
